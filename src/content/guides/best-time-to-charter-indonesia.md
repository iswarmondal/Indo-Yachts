Indonesia stretches across 5,000 kilometers of equatorial ocean. The sailing conditions in Komodo bear almost no resemblance to those in Raja Ampat, and the Banda Sea operates on an entirely different calendar from the Mentawai Islands. Understanding the seasons is not optional — it is the foundation of a great charter.

## The Two Monsoons

Indonesia's climate is governed by two monsoon systems:

**Northwest Monsoon (November–March):** Brings wetter conditions to western Indonesia and calmer seas to the east. This is prime season for Raja Ampat and the Banda Sea.

**Southeast Monsoon (April–October):** Drier conditions across most of the archipelago. This is the classic Komodo sailing season, and the period when the Spice Islands become accessible.

## Region by Region

### Raja Ampat — October to April

The eastern archipelago is at its finest during the northwest monsoon. Seas flatten, visibility improves dramatically, and manta ray aggregations peak between November and January. Water temperatures hover around 28–30°C year-round.

### Komodo — April to November

The dry season transforms the Komodo National Park into an ideal charter ground. Reliable winds from the southeast create excellent sailing conditions. Water temperatures drop slightly (25–27°C), which triggers nutrient upwelling and attracts mantas to sites like Mawan and Cauldron.

### The Banda Sea — October to November

The narrow window between monsoons produces the Banda Sea's legendary flat-calm conditions. This is one of the most exclusive charter routes in the world — crossing open ocean between volcanic islands, visiting nutmeg plantations, and diving pristine walls that plunge into abyssal depths.

### Mentawai Islands — April to October

Off Sumatra's west coast, the Mentawai chain offers world-class surf and untouched reef systems. The dry season brings consistent swells from the south and southwest. Luxury surf charters operate primarily from May through September.

## Water Temperature and Visibility

Across the archipelago, surface temperatures range from 25°C to 30°C depending on season and location. Visibility varies enormously:

- **Raja Ampat:** 15–35m, best November–January
- **Komodo:** 10–25m, best August–October
- **Banda Sea:** 25–40m during the October–November window
- **Alor and Flores:** 15–30m, consistent through the dry season

## Planning Around the Calendar

The most discerning charter guests plan 12 to 18 months in advance, particularly for peak-season Raja Ampat and the limited Banda Sea crossing windows. Vessels position themselves seasonally — a yacht in Komodo in July will typically be in Raja Ampat by November.

This seasonal migration is part of the rhythm of Indonesian chartering, and understanding it allows you to follow the very best conditions throughout the year.
