A luxury yacht charter in Indonesia is unlike any other travel experience. There are no check-in desks, no hotel lobbies, no tourist queues. From the moment you step aboard, the entire vessel — its crew, its chef, its itinerary — exists solely for you and your guests.

For those considering their first charter, this guide covers everything you need to know.

## Choosing the Right Vessel

Indonesian expedition yachts fall into several categories:

**Traditional Phinisi:** Handcrafted wooden sailing vessels inspired by centuries-old Bugis shipbuilding traditions. Modern phinisi yachts combine traditional aesthetics with contemporary luxury — air-conditioned cabins, professional dive facilities, and gourmet galleys.

**Motor Yachts:** Purpose-built steel or aluminum vessels offering greater range, stability, and speed. Ideal for itineraries that cover significant distances, such as Banda Sea crossings or extended Raja Ampat expeditions.

**Expedition Catamarans:** A newer category offering exceptional stability, shallow draft for reef anchorages, and spacious deck layouts. Well suited to families and groups prioritizing comfort.

## What Is Included

A fully crewed luxury charter typically includes:

- **All meals and non-alcoholic beverages** prepared by an onboard chef
- **Fuel and vessel operating costs** for the duration of the charter
- **Water sports equipment** — snorkeling gear, kayaks, paddleboards
- **Dive equipment and guided dives** (on dive-equipped vessels)
- **Crew gratuity guidance** provided by your charter broker

Items typically arranged separately include flights, premium wines and spirits, marine park permits, and specialized activities such as helicopter transfers or private island dining.

## Group Size and Cabin Configuration

Most luxury charter vessels in Indonesia accommodate **8 to 16 guests** across 4 to 8 cabins. The ideal group size for a private charter is 6 to 12 guests — large enough to share the experience, intimate enough that the vessel never feels crowded.

Cabins are typically configured as doubles or twins, with master suites featuring panoramic windows, en-suite bathrooms, and dedicated lounge areas.

## The Role of the Charter Broker

A knowledgeable charter broker serves as your advocate throughout the process:

1. **Vessel selection** matched to your group's priorities — diving, family, photography, exploration
2. **Itinerary design** based on season, conditions, and your interests
3. **Contract and logistics** — charter agreements, payment schedules, travel coordination
4. **Onboard preferences** — dietary requirements, celebration arrangements, activity planning

The broker relationship continues through embarkation and beyond, ensuring every detail is addressed before you step aboard.

## What to Pack

Indonesian charter guests should plan for:

- **Light, breathable clothing** — linens and cottons in neutral tones
- **Reef-safe sunscreen** — required in all marine park areas
- **Underwater camera** — the marine life demands documentation
- **Light rain jacket** — tropical showers pass quickly but arrive without warning
- **Minimal formal wear** — one or two elevated pieces for sunset dinners on deck

Leave heavy luggage behind. Soft bags stow far more easily than rigid suitcases, and the onboard atmosphere is deliberately relaxed.

## Your First Day Aboard

The charter experience begins with a tender transfer from port or airport to the vessel at anchor. You will be welcomed by the captain and crew, shown to your cabin, and briefed on safety procedures and the week's itinerary.

Within an hour, the vessel is underway. The port recedes. The first anchorage appears. And the pace of life shifts from the world you left behind to something altogether different.
